History of Superman

Superman was created by Jerry Siegel and Joe Shuster and first appeared in Action Comics #1 in 1938. He has since become one of the most iconic and recognizable superheroes in the world.

Superman's alter ego is Clark Kent, a mild-mannered reporter for the Daily Planet. He has many superhuman abilities, including super strength, speed, and the ability to fly. He is also invulnerable to most forms of physical harm.

Comics

Superman has been featured in countless comic book series, including Action Comics, Superman, and Justice League. Some of his most famous storylines include "The Death of Superman" and "Red Son."

Movies

Superman has been featured in several movies, including Superman: The Movie, Man of Steel, and Justice League. He has been portrayed by actors such as Christopher Reeve, Henry Cavill, and Brandon Routh.
